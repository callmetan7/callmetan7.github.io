The math

A rose is simply a set of points in polar coordinates. To convert from polar coordinates to the normal Cartesian coordinates we use the following equation. Since rose coordinates are specified using the cosine or sine function, roses are usually expressed as polar coordinate (rather than Cartesian coordinate) graphs of sinusoids that have angular frequency of kk k and an amplitude of aaa that determine the radial coordinate (r)(r)(r) given the polar angle (θ)(\theta)(θ) (though when kkk is a rational number, a rose curve can be expressed in Cartesian coordinates since those can be specified as algebraic curves).

x=cos⁡(θ)x = \cos(\theta) x=cos(θ) y=sin⁡(θ)y = \sin(\theta) y=sin(θ)

You can also use the normal Polar Coordinates if thats what your comfortable with using the following equation

r=acos⁡(θk)r = a\cos(\theta k) r=acos(θk)
