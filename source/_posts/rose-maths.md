# Polar Coordinates

- Polar coordinates are a different way of plotting points. Instead of \\((x, y)\\), we plot \\((r, \theta)\\)
- \\(r\\) is our radius or how far away we are from the origin
- \\(\theta\\) is an angle away from the x-axis
  ![Polar Coordinates Example](https://www.math.drexel.edu/~dp399/math122/pol_rect_conv.png)

- Since we create a right triangle, we can use trigonometric functions to convert from polar to cartesian

$$ x = rcos(\theta)$$ $$y = rsin(\theta) $$

---

Now that you know how to convert what happens if we start messing with the formulas!

### What if we multiply our input by some value?

$$ x = rcos(k\theta) $$ $$ y = rsin(k\theta) $$

\\(k\\) is a random constant

**We get flowers**
