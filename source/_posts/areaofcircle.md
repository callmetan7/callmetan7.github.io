## Area of a circle

Throughout our entire lives we have been told that \\(A=\pi r^2\\) but how did we arrive to this conclusion?

When this formula was invented the only thing we knew about was that the circumference was the ratio of pi to the diameter.

### Changing our way of thinking

If we change how we think about circles for a brief moment then we can see why \\(\pi r^2\\) is the area of a circle.

If we divide the circle into strips of rectangles that go around the center and lay them out flat we get a triangle!

{% asset_img Circle.jpeg %}

The largest strip is our circumference which we know as \\(2\pi r\\), while the shorter ends of all the rectangles combined is our radius as I labeled.

### Finding the Area of the Triangle

{% asset_img TriangleCircle.jpeg %}

We already know how to find the area of a triangle which is \\(\frac{1}{2}bh\\).
We now substitute our values to get the equation

$$ \frac{1}{2}(2\pi r)(r) $$

Which then finally simplifies into what all know and love

$$ A = \pi r^2 $$
